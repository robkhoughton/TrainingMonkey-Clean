## The Science Behind It

### **The Training Monkey Index (TMI)**
Our patent-pending four-dimensional breakthrough combining work:load and acute:chronic relationships:

```
TMI = (External Work ACWR - Internal Load ACWR) / ((External + Internal) / 2)
```

Where:
- **External Work ACWR**: Mechanical demands (distance + elevation) comparing 7-day to 28-day
- **Internal Load ACWR**: Physiological stress (heart rate TRIMP) comparing 7-day to 28-day

**This normalized divergence reveals the hidden mismatch** between what you're doing and how your body is responding‚Äîthe key insight missing from every other platform.

### **Supporting Metrics**

**TRIMP (Training Impulse)**: Uses the Banister formula to quantify training load based on duration and heart rate reserve. We calculate this using your actual heart rate stream, not smoothed averages.

**Acute:Chronic Work/Load Ratios**: Compares your recent training (7 days) to your chronic baseline (28 days). Above 1.5 = high injury risk. We calculate both external work and internal load ratios separately.

**Elevation Load Miles**: Research-validated conversion where 750 feet of climbing = 1 mile equivalent for running. This accounts for the increased energy cost of uphill running that distance alone misses.
