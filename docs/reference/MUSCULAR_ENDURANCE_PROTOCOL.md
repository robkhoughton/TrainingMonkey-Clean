# Muscular Endurance Training Protocol

**Source:** [Evoke Endurance — Muscular Endurance: All You Need to Know](https://evokeendurance.com/resources/muscular-endurance-all-you-need-to-know/)  
**Coaching context:** [[muscular_endurance]]

**Target athlete:** Trail/mountain runners with an established aerobic base (AeT within 10% of AnT HR)

---

## Two Training Methods

### Method 1 — Weighted Carries (field-based)

- **Terrain:** 30%+ grade, fire stairs, or Stairmaster
- **Load:** Start at ~10% bodyweight; increase until legs burn before breathing limits
- **Duration:** 30 min → up to 1 hour of total climbing
- Disregard heart rate; chase local muscle fatigue (leg burn)

### Method 2 — Gym Circuit (14-week block)

- **Frequency:** 1x/week (2x if overall volume is low)

**Warm-up:** 10–15 min aerobic to Zone 3 + 10 get-ups + 10 burpees

**Core exercises** (6 sets × 10 reps, 1 rep/sec tempo):

1. Split jump squats
2. Squat jumps
3. Box step-ups (box = 75% knee height)
4. Front lunges (40–60cm range)

*Added from Workout 5:*

- Goblet squat/overhead press (5×10)
- 2-hand kettlebell swings (5×10)

**Cool-down:** 10 min easy aerobic

---

## Progressive Load & Rest (14 weeks)

| Workout | Sets | Load | Rest/Set | Rest Between Exercises |
|---------|------|------|----------|------------------------|
| 1–2 | 6 | Bodyweight | 60s | 60s |
| 3 | 6 | BW | 45s | 60s |
| 4 | 5 | +10% | 60s | 60s |
| 5 | 6 | +10% | 45s | 90s |
| 6 | 6 | +10% | 40s | 60s |
| 7 | 6 | +10% | 30s | 60s |
| 8 | 8 | +10% | 45s | 60s |
| 9 | 6 | +15% | 40s | 45s |
| 10 | 8 | +15% | 45s | 30s |
| 11 | 8 | +15% | 30s | 30s |
| 12 | 8 | +15% | 15s | 20s |
| 13–14 | 8 | +15% | 10s | 10s |

The progression simultaneously adds sets, adds load, and cuts rest — that's the stimulus.

---

## Rules

- **Minimum 8 sessions** to see benefit; miss one → restart 2 workouts back
- **Soreness is expected** — first 2–3 sessions: use 80% range of motion and 4 sets instead of 6
- The burn is mandatory — if you're breathing hard before your legs burn, reduce load
- ME supplements aerobic base; Zone 1–2 volume must be maintained throughout

---

## When to Run This Block

Mountain runners: **early base period**, up to 16 weeks out from taper — then transition to uphill/rolling intervals 8 weeks pre-taper.

---

## Background: Why It Works

ME targets **Type IIa (fast-twitch with endurance capacity)** fibers. Slow-twitch fibers act as an "aerobic vacuum cleaner," clearing lactate produced by the working fast-twitch fibers. The combination of a big aerobic base + ME block covers ~90% of the gains available from structured mountain training.
