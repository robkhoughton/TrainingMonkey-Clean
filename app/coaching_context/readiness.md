# ANS READINESS INTERPRETATION

## Basis
Readiness is assessed via z-scores against personal rolling baselines — not population norms.
- HRV: 7-day acute vs 30-day chronic (std floor 3ms)
- RHR: 3-day acute vs 60-day chronic (std floor 1.5bpm)
Personal baseline only — population comparisons are invalid for masters athletes.

## State Definitions

**YELLOW_SYMPATHETIC — Acute Fatigue**
- Signal: HRV z ≤ -1.0 OR RHR z ≥ 1.0 OR combined (hrv_z − rhr_z) < -1.2
- Interpretation: Normal adaptive response to hard training, poor sleep, or life stress. Expected after hard sessions.
- Action: Zone 1–2 only. Defer planned intensity 24h — do not skip movement entirely. Easy effort accelerates clearance.

**YELLOW_PARASYMPATHETIC — Deep Hole**
- Signal: HRV z ≥ 1.5 AND RHR z ≤ -1.0 simultaneously
- Interpretation: COUNTER-INTUITIVE. Looks like peak readiness; is actually systemic exhaustion. Parasympathetic override = forced recovery. Do not trust subjective "feel good" in this state.
- Action: Rest or active recovery only. No intensity. No exceptions.

**RED — Systemic Overreaching**
- Signal: Both HRV suppressed (z ≤ -1.0) AND RHR elevated (z ≥ 1.0) on each of the last 4 consecutive days
- Interpretation: Fatigue accumulating faster than clearing. Non-functional overreaching threshold.
- Action: Mandatory deload. Volume cut ≥40%, eliminate all intensity. Reassess after minimum 3 days.

## Contextual Modifiers (Trail Running, Masters Athlete)
- Altitude: RHR naturally +3–5 bpm, HRV naturally -5–10ms at elevation. Do not classify as fatigued without other corroborating signals.
- Long descents: CNS/muscular recovery lags cardiovascular by 24–72h. HRV may not reflect full systemic status until 48h post long descent effort.
- Age (60s): Recovery tail is 1.5–2× longer than standard models. Expect 48–72h for HRV to reflect recovery after hard sessions, not 24h.
- Sleep deficit: Single night of poor sleep can suppress HRV and elevate RHR. Pair with subjective soreness before escalating to Yellow/Red classification.
- Dehydration: Weight drop ≥2% from 7-day average, elevated respiration rate, or low SpO2 can elevate RHR and suppress HRV independent of training fatigue. If these signals are present alongside a borderline Yellow reading, attribute the RHR elevation to dehydration first — action is rehydrate and reassess, not reduce intensity.
