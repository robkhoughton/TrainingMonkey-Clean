# STRENGTH TRAINING INTEGRATION

## Trap Bar Deadlift — Primary Lift
- Primary benefit: hip extension power, eccentric hamstring/glute load capacity for descents.
- Trap bar preferred over straight bar: reduces lumbar shear, increases quad recruitment, mimics climbing push mechanics.
- Periodize with trail load: heavy gym weeks should pair with lower trail volume weeks.
- Timing in week: perform at week start, not pre-run same day or post long-run day.

## Eccentric Overlap — Scheduling Rules
- Deadlifts + long descents within 48h = compounded DOMS and CNS fatigue. Avoid.
- Schedule heavy strength 2 days before OR 2 days after long descent efforts.
- No heavy loading within 72h of a target race.

## Load Classification
- Heavy leg session (≥80% 1RM): treat as a hard training day. Do not follow with trail intensity.
- Moderate strength (60–70% 1RM): compatible with easy trail same day or next day.
- Bodyweight/stability work: negligible trail load impact; can be done any day.

## Taper Protocol
- Reduce strength volume 3 weeks out from race.
- Maintain light strength (60% 1RM, reduced volume) through week 2 of taper to preserve neuromuscular activation.
- Stop all heavy loading 10 days before race.

## Trail-Specific Strength Priority (Ranked)
1. Single-leg stability (single-leg squat progressions) — ankle/knee injury prevention on uneven terrain
2. Posterior chain (trap bar deadlift, Romanian deadlift) — climbing power and descent eccentric control
3. Hip flexor mobility — stride length on climbs, technical terrain clearance
4. Core anti-rotation — lumbar protection on uneven footing and lateral trail features
