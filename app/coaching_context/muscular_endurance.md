# MUSCULAR ENDURANCE TRAINING — PROTOCOL

**Reference doc:** [[MUSCULAR_ENDURANCE_PROTOCOL]]

## Definition

Muscular Endurance (ME) = ability of a muscle to exert a relatively high % of max force for many repetitions.  
Target fiber: Type IIa (fast-twitch with aerobic capacity). Slow-twitch fibers clear the lactate byproduct aerobically.

## Prerequisite

AeT within 10% of AnT HR — aerobic base must be established before ME block begins.

## Method 1 — Weighted Carries (field)

- Grade: 30%+ (fire stairs or Stairmaster acceptable)
- Load: ~10% bodyweight to start; increase until legs fatigue before breathing limits
- Duration: 30 min → up to 1 hour climbing
- HR is irrelevant — local muscle burn is the target signal

## Method 2 — Gym Circuit (14-week block, 1x/week)

**Warm-up:** 10–15 min aerobic to Zone 3 + 10 get-ups + 10 burpees

**Core exercises** (1 rep/sec tempo):
1. Split jump squats
2. Squat jumps
3. Box step-ups (box = 75% knee height)
4. Front lunges (40–60cm range)

*From Workout 5 add:*
- Goblet squat/overhead press
- 2-hand kettlebell swings

**Cool-down:** 10 min easy aerobic

## Key Rules

- Minimum 8 sessions for benefit; miss one → restart 2 workouts back
- First 2–3 sessions: 4 sets only, 80% range of motion — expect 2-day DOMS
- Local muscle burn is mandatory; if breathing limits first, reduce load
- Zone 1–2 aerobic volume must be maintained throughout the block

## Why Muscular Endurance Limits Ultra Performance

In long trail efforts, performance degrades because local muscles fail before the aerobic system does. The limiting factor is the ability of the working muscles to sustain force output, not oxygen delivery. This is why ME training — not more aerobic volume — is the correct intervention when an athlete fades late in races despite adequate aerobic fitness.

## Placement in Training Year

- Mountain runners: early base period, up to 16 weeks pre-taper
- Transition to uphill/rolling intervals 8 weeks pre-taper
- ME supplements aerobic base — never replaces it
