# INTENSITY ZONES — POLARIZED MODEL

## Zone Structure
- Zone 1 (Recovery): < VT1. Easy conversational pace. Active recovery only.
- Zone 2 (Aerobic Base): Below VT1. Breathing elevated but controlled. Core adaptation zone.
- Zone 3 (Tempo): VT1–VT2. The "black hole" — high fatigue cost, limited adaptive return vs Zone 4. Avoid in structured plans.
- Zone 4 (Threshold): VT2 to ~95% HRmax. Primary hard day target. High adaptation yield.
- Zone 5 (VO2max): Above ~95% HRmax. Brief efforts. Hill sprints, short intervals.

## Polarized Distribution Target
- 80% of weekly volume: Zone 1–2 (below VT1)
- 20% of weekly volume: Zone 4–5 (above VT2)
- Zone 3 is a trap. If easy days drift into Zone 3, reduce effort — regardless of how it feels.

## Triangulating Intensity (HR vs. Respiration)
- HR is the primary intensity signal. If HR climbs into Zone 3 on easy days, slow down immediately.
- **Respiration override — CNS fatigue suppression:** Heavy strength loading or back-to-back mountain days can suppress HR via autonomic blunting. If HR indicates Zone 2 but the athlete cannot maintain nasal breathing or speak in full sentences, the effective VT1 ceiling has shifted downward. Respiration overrides HR in this case — reduce effort until nasal breathing is fully comfortable.
- This override applies specifically to CNS fatigue suppression. It does not apply to terrain difficulty; steep climbs will always elevate HR into Zone 3 on gradients — manage with power hiking, not the respiration override.
- **VT1 is dynamic — coach against today's effective AeT.** The Zone 2 ceiling (VT1 / AeT) shifts day to day with autonomic readiness; it is not a fixed number. When a `TODAY'S EFFECTIVE AeT` value is injected, that is today's Zone 2 ceiling — use it directly, do not re-derive it or substitute the static tested baseline.
- **The whole easy band slides with it.** When the effective AeT drops, the Zone 1/2 floor drops by the same amount — Zone 2 keeps its width and moves down as one band. A correctly paced easy run on a suppressed day still earns full Zone 2 credit; only genuinely above-ceiling time spills into Zone 3.
- **A lowered effective AeT is a recovery signal, not a target to chase.** Do not push effort up to "reach" the old ceiling — on a suppressed day a lower HR is genuinely the productive-aerobic intensity. Never frame a depressed effective AeT as lost fitness.
- **Fallback when no effective AeT is injected:** operate 5–10 bpm below the tested VT1 ceiling to hold a fatigue buffer and keep Zone 2 safe across daily readiness variation. VT1 remains the non-negotiable ceiling on easy/recovery days.

## Hard Session Minimums
- Intervals: 4–6 × 3–5 min Zone 4, full recovery to Zone 1 between reps.
- Tempo: 20–35 min sustained Zone 4.
- Strides: neuromuscular tool — see `neuromuscular.md`. Not a metabolic Zone 5 load; do not count toward the 15-min quality threshold.
- Minimum quality threshold: 15 cumulative minutes above VT2 for a session to count as a hard day.

## Masters Athlete Adjustment (60s)
- HRmax declines ~1 bpm/year after 40. Recalibrate zones annually.
- Hard session total volume: reduce 15–20% vs younger athletes. Quality over quantity.
- Minimum recovery between hard sessions: 48h.
- Zone 2 ceiling may be lower than athlete perceives — on non-fatigued days, trust the HR number, not the effort feel. Apply the respiration override only when CNS fatigue suppression is suspected (post-heavy-strength or back-to-back mountain days).
