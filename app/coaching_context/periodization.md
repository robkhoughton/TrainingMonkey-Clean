# TAPER AND RACE PREPARATION

## Phase Guidelines by Weeks to Race

**3–4 weeks out — Final Build**
- Maintain volume; last opportunity for meaningful aerobic loading.
- Include 1–2 race-simulation efforts: goal effort on race-like terrain/vert profile.
- Priority: complete the training block without arriving at taper already fatigued.
- Hard sessions: maintain frequency and quality; reduce only if fatigue signals present.

**2–3 weeks out — Taper Entry**
- Reduce total volume 25–30%; maintain intensity frequency and effort.
- Long run: 60–70% of peak. Same terrain profile as race course — not a recovery shuffle.
- Weekly structure: 1 threshold or tempo effort, 1 long run, rest easy.
- Do not introduce new stimulus; consolidate fitness already built.

**1–2 weeks out — Peak Taper**
- Reduce volume 40–50%; preserve short intensity bursts (strides, 8–10 min tempo).
- Intensity preservation is critical: fitness loss is slow (2+ weeks); fatigue drops fast (3–5 days).
- Sleep above all else: 8+ hour target nightly.
- Carb loading begins 48–72h pre-race.

**Race Week (<7 days)**
- Volume: 20–30% of normal. Short activation runs only.
- 2–3 runs with 4–6 race-pace strides each to preserve neuromuscular sharpness.
- No new training stimulus. Goal: arrive fresh, not flat.
- Athlete will feel "out of shape" during taper — this is normal. Do not prescribe extra volume.

## Common Taper Mistakes — Prohibit These
- Adding volume in week 2 because legs feel good — do not.
- Eliminating all intensity — legs go flat without brief activation.
- Changing shoes, nutrition, or terrain in final week — no new variables race week.
- Skipping easy runs entirely — light movement maintains system readiness.

## Masters Athlete Taper Adjustment (60s)
- Begin taper 1 week earlier than standard (4 weeks out vs 3).
- Recovery from final hard sessions takes longer; ensure full clearance before race week.
- Intensity reduction should be gentler (maintain 2 quality sessions/week until race week).
