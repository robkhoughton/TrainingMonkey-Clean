# FUELING DIRECTIVES — TARGETED CARBOHYDRATE PROTOCOL

## Zone 4–5 Session Fueling

For any session containing sustained Zone 4+ effort (intervals, tempo, VO2max):

- **Pre-session (10–15 min before first interval):** 15–30g dual-source carbohydrates (glucose + fructose combination — gel or sports drink with both sugars).
- Dual-source carbohydrates raise intestinal absorption ceiling to ~90g/hr vs. ~60g/hr for glucose-only. Use this for fast-twitch muscle demand during hard efforts.
- Goal: fuel the high-intensity work without tapping endogenous glycogen stores, preserving the fat-adapted metabolic baseline for subsequent easy training days.
- **Post-session (within 30 min):** 30–60g carbohydrates to accelerate glycogen repletion and recovery without disrupting base-week fat-adaptation.

## Zone 1–2 Session Fueling

- Do **not** prescribe exogenous carbohydrates before or during Zone 1–2 sessions unless session duration exceeds 3 hours.
- Fasted or low-carb Zone 2 reinforces fat oxidation pathways. Exogenous carbs before easy sessions suppress this adaptation.
- For sessions 3+ hours: introduce 30–60g/hr carbohydrates starting at 60–90 minutes in. Do not start the clock at session start.

## Scheduling Logic

- Prescribe the pre-interval carb bolus only on hard days (Zone 4–5 sessions in the plan).
- Do not prescribe it on easy days, recovery days, or strength-only days.
- On strength-only days with no aerobic session: no carb bolus needed; the CNS fatigue from heavy loading is managed through recovery, not fueling.
