# ZONE 2 TRAINING — AEROBIC BASE PROTOCOL

**Reference doc:** [[ZONE_2_PROTOCOL]]

## Definition

Zone 2 ceiling = Aerobic Threshold (AeT) = blood lactate ~2 mMol/L.  
This is the same as VT1 / the top of the polarized easy band.

## AeT Is Dynamic — Use Today's Effective Value

The Zone 2 ceiling is not a fixed number. A calibrated baseline (from a drift or LT1 test) is modulated each day by autonomic readiness into **today's effective AeT**. When a `TODAY'S EFFECTIVE AeT` value is injected, it — not the static tested baseline — is today's Zone 2 ceiling. Use it directly; do not re-derive it.

- The entire easy band slides with it: when the effective AeT drops, the Zone 1/2 floor drops by the same amount, so Zone 2 keeps its width. A correctly paced easy run on a suppressed day still accumulates full Zone 2 time.
- A depressed effective AeT is a recovery signal — do not raise effort to reach the old ceiling; the lower HR is genuinely today's productive-aerobic intensity.
- The aerobic-base target is productive Zone 2 time against the slid band, not Zone 1. A slow walk below the floor is not a mitochondrial stimulus and does not substitute.

## Intensity — How to Verify

- **Gold standard:** HR Drift Test (anchors to AeT directly) — establishes the *ideal-state* VT1 ceiling, not the daily mandatory target
- **Field check:** upper limit of comfortable nose breathing; can hold a full conversation
- **Do not use** % of HRmax — individual accuracy is too low to be reliable
- If AeT is within 10% of AnT HR, aerobic base is well-developed

## Session Guidelines

- Duration: 30 min minimum; up to several hours
- Duration is the primary adaptation multiplier — longer sessions at correct intensity outperform shorter ones
- Intensity must stay at or below AeT; drifting above negates the aerobic stimulus and adds fatigue cost

## Volume Benchmarks

- Recreational athletes: majority of weekly volume (high Zone 1–2 %)
- Well-trained athletes: ~80% of total volume in Zone 1–2 (polarized model)
- Elite marathoners run only ~10–15% of volume in Zone 2 — but their Zone 2 pace (~4:40/mile) carries extreme neuromuscular load; this is fatigue management, not a low-base recommendation

## Physiological Target

- Builds mitochondrial density, aerobic enzyme capacity, capillary density in slow-twitch fibers
- Develops the lactate shuttle: fast-twitch fibers produce lactate → slow-twitch fibers oxidize it aerobically
- **FatMax occurs in the middle of Zone 2, not at the ceiling.** Riding the absolute top of the zone does not yield superior aerobic adaptations but increases sympathetic stress. Target the middle of the zone, not the VT1 ceiling.
- Adaptations accumulate over months to years; cannot be rushed

## Common Coaching Errors to Avoid

- Prescribing Zone 2 that has drifted into Zone 3 ("black hole") — high fatigue, low return
- Telling a fit athlete Zone 2 "should feel easy" — at high fitness, correct Zone 2 pace is genuinely challenging
- Copying elite volume distributions without accounting for neuromuscular load differences
- Prioritizing HIIT over base in athletes with Aerobic Deficiency Syndrome (underdeveloped aerobic base from excessive high-intensity history)

## Relationship to Hard Days

Zone 2 is the foundation. HIIT is a supplement — it amplifies an existing aerobic base, it does not replace one.  
An athlete without adequate Zone 2 base gets: fewer reps per session, slower recovery, higher injury risk, compressed adaptation ceiling.
